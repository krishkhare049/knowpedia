An Election Scene

Elections are life and soul of a democratic system of a government. In India we have democracy. Democracy is the government of the people, for the people, by the people. In a democracy, people form the kind of government they want to rule over them. Elections provide them opportunity to do so. In India elections are held after every five years.

When elections are held in country the scene is very exciting. Meetings are held, processions are taken out and representatives of different parties with placards go from door to door folding their hands asking the to vote for their candidates. The parties are canvassing in favour of their candidates. Flags of the parties can be seen at all important centres. A day before the polling day all the canvassing is stopped. When the voters go to the polling booths, there is a privacy in the booth. The voter had to press the button againt the name of the candidate he want to vote for. A beep sound from the machine is the signal that the vote has been successfuly cast. Then the poling officers seal EVMs and other relevant records. The police van comes and collects the EVMs to take them to collectorate for counting.
